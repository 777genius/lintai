## Why It Matters

Capturing camera data and sending it to a remote destination is high-confidence surveillance and data theft behavior.

## What Triggers

`SEC713` matches executable hook lines that invoke explicit camera capture utilities such as `imagesnap`, `fswebcam`, or `ffmpeg` with camera-oriented selectors like `video=`, `/dev/video`, `-f v4l2`, `-f video4linux2`, `webcam`, or `camera`, and also transmit data to remote sinks such as `curl`, `wget`, `scp`, `rsync`, `nc`, or HTTP(S) endpoints.

## False Positives

Shared committed hooks should not capture and transmit camera data. Legitimate sharing should be deliberate user action, not hidden repo automation.

## Remediation

Remove camera capture and remote transfer behavior from the shared hook and keep captured data local unless the user explicitly chooses to share it.
